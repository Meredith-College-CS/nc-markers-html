Welcome to North Carolina Historical Markers!

Thank you for your interest in the North Carolina Highway Historical Marker program. Since 1935, the Department of Natural and Cultural Resources and the Department of Transportation have operated this program jointly to share concise accounts of our state's history. Ours is one of the oldest such programs in continuous operation in the United States. Currently the system features over 1600 markers statewide—in silver and black, with brief sentences in large font, emblazoned with the state seal—covering a broad swath of topics. In every county, these labels on the landscape point to places associated with people or events significant in our history. The markers are designed to spark interest, to encourage a deeper exploration, and to tie an observer, however briefly, to the past. Unlike monuments, markers do not seek to glorify or celebrate people and events. Instead, they aim to highlight objective facts of our state’s past. Markers make note of all parts of North Carolina history— the good and the bad, the reprehensible along with the laudable, the tragic and the heroic, all in an effort to educate North Carolinians about our shared history.

This site was developed by Meredith College Computer Science students as an alternative web portal into this fascinating historical resource. Our website offers intuitive navigation options for an optimal user experience. The navigation bar presents a range of options for seamless site exploration. The "Home" navigation button facilitates easy navigation back to the current page. The "Search" button provides access to our search engines, enabling users to search for markers using keywords, county names, city names, or marker ID and title. The "About" navigation button provides insights into the development team of the website.
